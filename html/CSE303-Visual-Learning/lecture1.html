Mean vs Median vs Mode

Understand statistics visually with animations and skewed graphs

1. Mean (Average)

Mean is the average value. We add all numbers and divide by total quantity.

Mean = (Sum of all values) / (Total number of values)
Example: 10, 12, 13, 14, 100

Total = 149
Count = 5

Mean = 149 / 5 = 29.8

⚠ Problem with Mean

One extreme value (called an outlier) can pull the mean far away from the real center. Here, most numbers are around 10-14, but because of 100, the mean becomes 29.8 which is misleading.

2. Median

Median is the middle value after arranging numbers in order.

Median = Middle Number
Example: 10, 12, 13, 14, 100

Middle value = 13

✅ Why Median is Better

Median ignores the effect of extreme outliers. Even though 100 is very large, the median stays near the actual center. That is why median is better for skewed data like salaries, house prices, or income distribution.

3. Mode

Mode is the value that appears most frequently.

Mode = Most Frequent Value
Example: 2, 3, 3, 3, 5, 6

Mode = 3

Normal Distribution

Perfectly balanced distribution. Both Mean and Median stay at the center.

Right Skewed Distribution

The long tail goes to the RIGHT. Large outliers pull the Mean toward the tail.

Left Skewed Distribution

The long tail goes to the LEFT. Small outliers pull the Mean toward the tail.

5. Quick Comparison

Mean

Uses every value.

Best when data has no outliers.

Median

Uses the middle value.

Best for skewed data and outliers.

Mode

Uses the most common value.

Useful for categories and popularity.
